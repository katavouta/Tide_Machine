Simulation of Kelvin's Tide Predicting Machine

This interactive module simulates a mechanical analog tide computer. Harmonic constituents are mechanically summed via an elegant system of wires and pulleys to draw an automated tidal curve in real time.

Horizontal lines on the coordinate chart are spaced 1m apart. The light vertical bars mark off individual 24-hour days. Tip: Click directly into the yellow cells in the table below to edit custom configuration values!

Origins of the Selected Constituents

  • M2 (Principal lunar semi-diurnal): Typically the dominant constituent forced by the rotation of the Earth relative to the Moon twice a day.
  • S2 (Principal solar semi-diurnal): The main solar component, pulsing every 12 hours, interacting with M2 to cause the spring-neap cycle.
  • N2 (Larger lunar elliptic semi-diurnal): Modulates the semi-diurnal tides to account for the monthly variation in lunar distance due to the elliptical shape of the Moon’s orbit.
  • K1 (Lunisolar diurnal): A major diurnal component accounting for declination variations of the Moon and Sun.
  • O1 (Principal lunar diurnal): A key diurnal constituent representing the changing north-south tilt of the Moon's orbit.
  • M4 (Shallow water overtide): Generated locally by non-linear distortion of the main lunar wave in shallow shelf seas.

Regional Features: Liverpool exhibits a large semi-diurnal tidal range dominated heavily by M2. Southampton is uniquely characterized by distorted shallow-water effects, giving rise to distinct double-high waters where shallow overtides like M4 interact prominently with the semi-diurnals.
